[THE PROBLEM] Millions of adults experience unmet mental health needs. What is the downstream economic impact of failing to provide care?
[THE DATA] We analyzed millions of federal records from SAMHSA/NSDUH—the gold standard for U.S. behavioral health data—utilizing complex statistical weighting.
[THE QUESTION] We were curious: Does an unmet mental health need directly destroy workforce participation? Exactly how severe is the detachment?
[THE HYPOTHESIS] Adults reporting unmet needs will have statistically higher odds of being fully unemployed or out of the labor force, regardless of demographics.
[THE TEST: ENGINEERING] We built robust PostgreSQL pipelines to harmonize massive schema drift, isolating precise demographics to eliminate covariant noise.
[THE TEST: STATISTICAL] We executed Chi-Square distributions to test for independence, proving the relationship between unmet need and unemployment is highly statistically significant (p < 0.05).
[THE FINDINGS] Unemployment spikes violently when an unmet need is present. Leaving mental health untreated is a measurable, catastrophic workforce crisis.
MODULE.02 // HYPOTHESIS SOURCE: NSDUH 2019
Primary Model
Adults reporting an unmet need for mental health treatment have higher odds of being unemployed or out of the labor force than adults without an unmet need, controlling for demographics.
  • Exposure: unmet_need (Yes/No)
  • Outcome: employment_status (Full-Time, Part-Time, Unemployed)
  • Covariates: age_group, sex, race_ethnicity, education_level
  • Weights: sample_weight (Statistical normalization)
MODULE.03 // DATA GOVERNANCE SYSTEM: PostgreSQL
Pipelines & Cleansing
This project included a heavy data-cleaning step focused on renaming raw public-use survey variables into plain English. Public datasets like NSDUH and TEDS-A use compact codebook names (AMHTXND2, IRWRKSTAT) which are efficient for documentation but encrypted and unreadable in downstream dashboards.

I standardized these fields into readable nomenclature so my SQL, Tableau workflows, and eventual presentations would be entirely transparent to non-technical stakeholders. This step strictly improved readability and cross-dataset consistency without ever changing the underlying data definitions.

MODULE.04 // STATISTICAL TESTS STATUS: VALIDATED
What The Data Shows Us
Rather than relying on raw counts, rigorous statistical validation was applied to prove the hypothesis.
  • 1. Contingency & Cross-Tabulation: Shows that within populations suffering from unmet mental health needs, the percentage of individuals capable of holding full-time employment drops significantly (from ~67% down to ~61%), shifting heavily toward part-time or total unemployment.
  • 2. Chi-Square Test of Independence: Statistically proves (Rejecting the Null Hypothesis) that employment status is intrinsically dependent on mental health treatment accessibility. The massive disparities observed are not random population anomalies.
  • 3. Modeling Progression: Initial logistic regression modeling failed because both primary vectors were categorical integers with no underlying continuous scale. This validated the necessity of advancing to Multinomial Logistic Regression to properly weigh the outcomes.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY // TL;DR THE BOTTOM LINE
The TL;DR Interpretation
Unmet Needs = Workforce Detachment When people do not have an unmet mental health need, the absolute vast majority are securely engaged in full-time work.

However, when people do suffer from an unmet mental health need, the rate of total unemployment and severe workforce detachment spikes substantially relative to their underlying population size.
